A waltz-time funk number is a genuinely unusual bet this deep into an album that has mostly trafficked in rock anthems and emotional weight, and 4Castle mostly wins it. The groove sits at a comfortable pace where the three-beat phrase feels less like a restriction and more like a slow roll, and the call-and-response chorus, 'I need to get my fix / of your sweet Pixy Stix,' lands with the kind of sticky confidence that early Kool and the Gang made look effortless. The saxophone tears through the back half with real personality, and the vocal split between lead and backups in the final chorus adds enough lift to push the whole thing home. The candy-as-desire conceit is well-worn territory, but the band plays it with enough wink and swing that it holds. Coming off the earnest introspection of 'Paradox of Contradictions,' this track's tonal left turn keeps the second half from settling into a groove the listener has already mapped.
