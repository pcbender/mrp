PCBender turns a breakup song inside out here, and the twist lands because the lyric earns it: the same chorus that opens as a wound, 'not long enough for me,' closes as a door slammed shut on the narrator's terms. The track moves at a steady 107 BPM in F# minor, grounded by a bassline that gives the groove more funk than the blues-rock tags suggest, and the production is clean enough that every shift in the story registers clearly. The real craft is structural: by the time a stranger shows up sharing a jacket in the rain, you feel the emotional center of gravity actually move, which is harder to pull off than it sounds. A highlight.
