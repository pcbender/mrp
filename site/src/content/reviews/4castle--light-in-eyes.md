In waltz time at 112 BPM, 'Light in Eyes' builds its central argument the way the best prog-rock hymns do: slowly, deliberately, until the weight of it lands. The lyric 'And sometimes in the deepest night / We envy those without the sight' reframes what first reads as a celebration of inner fire into something more honest and more earned, and 4Castle has the vocal firepower to carry that ambivalence without letting it collapse into mope. The guitar solo hits with the kind of emotionally weighted phrasing you get from David Gilmour on his better nights, technically capable but beholden to the song's mood rather than its own velocity. A highlight.
