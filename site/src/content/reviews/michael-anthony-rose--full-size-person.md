After a run of songs that have been asking careful, patient questions, 'Full-Size Person' is the moment the album stops asking and starts insisting. Rose has been circling the theme of dignity under pressure throughout this record, but this is where he puts a fist through the wall. The verses run tight and coiled before the choruses open up into something genuinely anthemic, a dynamic shift that earns every decibel. The lyric does the heavy lifting without flinching: 'don't mistake worn down for gone' lands like a clean punch, and the bridge's stripped-down repetition of 'less seen' before the final chorus hits as hard as anything John Hiatt wrote when he was at his most unguarded. In the context of the album, this is the emotional turning point: the second half of the record has been building toward a different kind of reckoning than the first half offered, one less about loss and more about what survives it. 'Full-Size Person' is where that reckoning arrives. Everything after it has a different weight because of what this track established.
