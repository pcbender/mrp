After the open-sky contemplation of 'The Dreamer and the Dreamed,' 'Double Black Diamonds' locks back into a confident country-rock groove and doesn't let go. The tempo lift feels natural rather than forced, and the female lead cresting over the full band on that refrain delivers payoff that the album has been building toward since the opener's dynamic restraint. 'Cold on my face, but my temperature's rising' earns its keep as a hook, nodding toward the anthemic clarity of early Heart without losing 4Castle's own fingerprints. At the album's midpoint, this is exactly the right kind of wide-open, windows-down track: it doesn't demand anything from the listener, it just delivers.
