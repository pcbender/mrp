Rose builds this one around a genuinely unusual conceit: Aletheion, daughter of Apa, walks into the Court of Opinion not to win an argument but to dissolve the habit of winning arguments, and the lyric earns that idea cleanly with the image of a string pulled tightly that 'will always answer back.' The song sits at a measured 107 BPM in E major, but it doesn't feel patient so much as coiled, guitar work that shifts from precise, palm-muted drive into melodic soloing that carries real expressive weight. The chorus resolves on 'resonance aligned with what is,' and Rose delivers the line with enough conviction that the philosophical abstraction lands as feeling rather than lecture. A highlight.
