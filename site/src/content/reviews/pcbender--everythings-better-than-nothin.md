After the anxiety of 'Confused by Your Wires,' the plain-spoken pragmatism of 'Everything's Better Than Nothin'' lands as genuine relief without feeling like a retreat. PCBender plants a flag squarely in the tradition of working-man rock philosophy, and the hook earns its keep: 'If being good ain't good enough / I'll take my chance with luck' carries the kind of unadorned weight you get from John Hiatt or early Tom Petty when they stopped trying to be clever. The shuffle groove at a deliberate mid-tempo clip keeps things driving without rushing, and the guitar solo hits like a genuine release rather than decoration. The anthemic chorus, stacked with background vocals pushing back against the lead, is the kind of thing that holds a room. In sequence, it functions as the album's first open exhale, and it earns that role without softening anything.
