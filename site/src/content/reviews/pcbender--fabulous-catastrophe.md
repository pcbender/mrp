PCBender leans into a classic rock groove here, a steady 99 BPM pulse in G major that lets the verses unspool like a good yarn before the chorus opens up into something genuinely communal. The hook earns its keep: 'two wild souls with strange harmony / no common sense, just chemistry' is the kind of couplet that sounds obvious in retrospect but lands with real warmth the first time through, especially when the background harmonies stack in and push the whole thing toward singalong territory. There's a looseness to the storytelling in the verses, lost keys in the fallen leaves, mismatched personalities finding their fit, that recalls John Hiatt at his most generous and unpretentious. A highlight.
