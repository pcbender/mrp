At 143 BPM in E minor, 'Inner Fire' earns its title: the opening builds with real purpose before the extended lead guitar solo kicks in around the 0:42 mark and simply takes over, all rapid-fire melodic runs and neo-classical authority that call to mind early Yngwie without the cold precision. The rhythm section stays locked and punchy throughout, giving the lead room to breathe and burn simultaneously. It's a showcase track, no question, and PCBender's phrasing has the kind of earned confidence you hear from players who've lived inside this tuning for decades.
