At the album's midpoint, 'Diode' arrives at a punishing pace and delivers the record's coldest thesis: the artist as signal, consumed and discarded by the machine he feeds. The processed robotic voice bookending the track is a smart production choice, framing the whole song as evidence of the very argument it makes, and the chorus 'I am the tool / Electric hands take what I give / but nothing feeds back' lands with a directness that sticks. The driving synth arpeggios push forward with mechanical relentlessness, and when the vocal cuts through on the hook, it soars in a way that recalls early Nine Inch Nails stripped of self-pity, replaced with something colder and more resigned. Four tracks of building tension and thematic accumulation have made this moment feel earned. 'Diode' is the album's emotional center of gravity, and what follows has to find a way out of the cold it creates.
