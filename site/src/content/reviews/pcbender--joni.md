'Joni' hits with the kind of direct sincerity that feels rare in rock songwriting, a friend's voice cutting through the noise, telling someone they're beautiful not because it's easy, but because it needs to be said. Coming straight off the wide-open rush of 'Two Tree Road,' it holds the tempo high and keeps the energy level, but it pivots the emotional register sharply: where the opener was about freedom and motion, this one is about showing up for someone. Built on a galloping foundation and anchored in a minor key, the track layers distorted guitars into a wall that never overwhelms the vocal; instead, it props up the tenderness of the message. PCBender's harmonic support and the technically assured guitar solo after the second chorus elevate what could have been a simple verse-chorus into something more architecturally sound. It's a song about choosing friendship over false fixes, and the music refuses to cheapen that choice with sentimentality. Back-to-back with 'Two Tree Road,' it confirms early that this album moves between emotional registers without losing its footing.
