PCBender has always known how to build a song from the ground up, and 'Then There Was Nothing' earns its seven-minute run time the hard way: by actually going somewhere. The shimmering, delay-soaked intro gives way to a chorus that hits like a wave breaking, distorted guitars and drums landing with the kind of cathartic weight you hear on the better end of classic rock radio. The lyric 'she didn't arrive, she was always there' does real philosophical work without straining for it, and the C major key keeps the whole thing open and unguarded even as the arrangement thickens. A highlight that holds the room.
