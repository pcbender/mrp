"The bridge gets you over / the rage of the river" is the kind of lyric that earns its chorus, and 4Castle delivers on the promise: the lift into that hook around the 1:24 mark hits like a door swinging open, the rhythm section locking into a groove that carries the whole emotional weight of the song. In D major at a steady near-100 BPM, the track builds patiently through its verses before the harmonies stack and the processed guitars open up into full arena mode, calling to mind the best of Heart or late-period Foreigner without feeling borrowed. "Crossing with a certain sense of uncertainty" lands as both the hook and the thesis, which is a rare trick to pull off.
