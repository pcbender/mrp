The drum solo that kicks off 'Pepper Pot' is a declaration of intent: this track means business from the first bar. At 112 BPM in B minor, the song locks into a tight, syncopated groove that carries PCBender's sharpest lyrical work in recent memory, the kind of writing where a line like 'every hand that learns to choose learns also how to overuse' lands with the blunt force of a good John Hiatt verse. The chorus image, a pepper pot going round and round, indifferent to its own diminishment, is the sort of central metaphor that earns repeat listens. A confident highlight.
