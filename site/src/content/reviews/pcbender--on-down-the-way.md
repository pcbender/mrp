PCBender opens 'On Down the Way' with just clean electric guitar and voice, and the patience of that choice pays off when the full band arrives around the 0:44 mark, a lift that feels genuinely earned. The waltz time at a brisk 129 BPM keeps things from turning mournful, grounding the grief in forward motion, which is exactly right for lyrics that trade in hard-won acceptance: 'I've made my peace with what won't stay / I'm moving still, and that's my grace.' The hook is simple and does exactly what it needs to do, landing in E major with the kind of plain-spoken resolve you hear in early John Hiatt or latter-day Springsteen at his most direct.
