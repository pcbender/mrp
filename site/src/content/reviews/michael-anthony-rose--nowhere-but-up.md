At 143 BPM in E minor, "Nowhere But Up" hits hard from the first bar and doesn't let up, with harmonized guitars stacked into a wall of sound that earns every bit of its triumphant tone. The hook is blunt in the best way: Rose repeats the title like a mantra, and by the third chorus it feels less like a lyric than a commitment. The line "let the fear fall into the dark" is the emotional hinge the verses build toward, and the production, punchy drums up front and melodic guitar solos cutting through the density, gives it the lift it needs. Fans of early arena-era Whitesnake or the more propulsive side of Collective Soul will find something familiar and satisfying here.
