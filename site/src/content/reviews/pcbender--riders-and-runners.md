The contrast does the heavy lifting here: clean arpeggiated guitars open each verse with a kind of coiled restraint, and then the chorus hits like a door coming off its hinges, distorted and wide, the rhythm section locking in hard at that 112 BPM clip. Lyrically, PCBender is in his element, 'stained glass reflections distorting the image' landing as the kind of line that earns its keep. The hook itself is sharp and repeatable, the riders-and-runners inversion giving it a folk-parable quality not far from Tom Petty at his most allegorical. A highlight.
