Following the album's emotional peak is a genuine structural challenge, and 'Rooms Keep People' meets it by shifting the subject without shifting the register. Where 'Some Things Just Stop' inventories what fades inside a person, this one turns outward, to the objects and spaces that hold the shape of who used to be there: the dent in the carpet where the table was, the bare coat hook, the mug that should have been thrown out. Rose writes grief the way John Prine wrote ordinary life, with specific, unshowy things doing the emotional heavy lifting. The lyric that stops you is the bridge: 'I used to think remembering was something I could choose / Now it's more like weather moving in and filling up the rooms.' That reframe earns everything the song has been building toward. The arrangement opens from finger-picked quiet into a chorus that swells without ever crowding the vocal, and that restraint is the production's best choice. As a follow-up to the album's centerpiece, it does not try to match it; it deepens it from a different angle, which is the smarter move.
