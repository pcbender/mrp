At 152 BPM in G minor and written in 3/4, 'Burn Me' moves with an urgency that shouldn't work as well as it does, a waltz-time engine driving something that feels more like a sprint. The chorus lands the way it needs to: 'fire and skin, a perfect fix' hits like a chant, and the production earns its density, with the guitars widening right on cue without burying the vocal. The bridge, 'single nights, endless fails / no one hears, no one calls,' is the quiet punch that reframes everything before the final chorus burns it down. A highlight, and one of STAB's most tightly constructed tracks.
