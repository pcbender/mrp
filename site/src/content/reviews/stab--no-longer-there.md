The bridge is where STAB earns it: the arrangement pulls back to something close to just piano and voice, and when the final chorus hits back in D major at a steady mid-tempo pulse, the line 'reaching for something that's no longer there' lands with real weight. The production keeps things clean without going sterile, and the vocal sits forward enough that you catch every shade of the performance. This is STAB's more introspective side, closer to early Sheryl Crow than The Runaways, but the craft is there.
