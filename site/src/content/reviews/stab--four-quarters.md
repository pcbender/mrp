Running at a brisk 152 BPM in D major, 'Four Quarters' builds like a ritual that actually works: the verses move as quiet incantation, then the chorus opens up into something genuinely cathartic, carried by that line 'to be the fool and let it go' landing exactly where it needs to. The tarot and elemental imagery could easily tip into self-seriousness, but STAB keeps it grounded by letting the dynamic swell do the heavy lifting rather than the concept. It sits somewhere between the melodic confidence of mid-period Bangles and the kind of driving anthem Stevie Nicks might have cut if she'd leaned harder into the rock side of things. A highlight.
