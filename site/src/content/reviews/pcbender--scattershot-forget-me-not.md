The title earns its keep: 'scattershot forget-me-not' is the kind of phrase that makes you rewind, and PCBender builds the whole song honestly around that idea, a love letter from someone who keeps fumbling the words but means every one of them. In E major at a clip that feels faster on paper than it does in the room, the track leans on a warm piano and a vocal that stays close and unguarded through the verses before the strings open things up at the chorus. The bridge lands the emotional gut-punch quietly: 'maybe my love's just a puzzle with half the pieces drawn / but you always filled in the rest.' A highlight.
