At 129 BPM in F minor, 'Just a Waste of Tears' runs colder and more controlled than anything STAB has put out recently, and that restraint is exactly what makes it land. The chorus hook, 'I'm lost within his wires / every time he disappears,' earns its repetition by the third time around, sitting in that space where emotional resignation and melodic insistence pull in opposite directions. The production keeps things clean and pressurized, a punchy low end holding the verses taut while the song opens up just enough on the chorus to let the grief breathe. This is STAB leaning into her more introspective side, and she sounds completely at home there.
