After 'I Think I'm Falling' turns the album inward and emotionally weighty, 'Deb' provides a welcome shift in register without losing any of the record's warmth. A good-natured portrait of a woman who lights up every room she enters, it earns its chorus with genuine feeling rather than sentimentality. PCBender rolls in at a comfortable mid-tempo pace and leans into that confidence the same way Tom Petty used to, letting the crunchy electric guitar do the heavy lifting while the vocal stays conversational and then opens up wide on 'the adventure unfolding each day of my life with Deb.' That chorus is the engine here, an anthemic lift that lands every time it comes around. The lyrical conceit is smart too, pairing the three-verse character study with single-word parenthetical labels like 'Intoxicating' and 'Prevaricating' that give Deb a knowing complexity without overstating it. Positioned between the album's emotional center and its final stretch, it functions as a palette cleanser that doesn't feel like filler; it feels like a breath of fresh air before the record makes its closing arguments.
