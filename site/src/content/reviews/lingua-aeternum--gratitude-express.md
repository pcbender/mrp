Lingua Aeternum stepping into straight-ahead anthemic rock is a genuine surprise, and 'Gratitude Express' earns it: a waltz-time ride at a brisk 152 BPM in D major, where arpeggiated guitar opens the track before the full band kicks in and the chorus simply lifts. 'No ticket, just a yes' lands as a hook because it's earned by the verse's setup, not just stated, and the sing-along 'Feelin' fine on the Gratitude Express' payoff has the same uncomplicated momentum you'd find in early Tom Petty at his most road-ready. The mood tags suggest melancholy, but this is mostly sunny side up, the 'small and bright / like lanterns in the night' bridge being the one moment where weight quietly acknowledges itself before the final chorus sweeps it forward.
