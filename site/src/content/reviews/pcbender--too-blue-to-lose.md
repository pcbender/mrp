PCBender plants this one firmly in E major and rides it hard, with a locked-in rhythm section pushing a surprisingly brisk pace for a blues lament and guitar work that earns its space from the first measure. The hook does something clever with contradiction: 'I'm so blue, I can't get losing right' reframes heartbreak as a kind of absurdist incompetence, and it lands harder every time through. The solos are full of fast, expressive bends in the best Chicago-to-Texas tradition, more Stevie Ray than B.B. but clearly fluent in both. A dependable cut that does its job and does it well.
