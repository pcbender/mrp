By the third track, the album has established a pattern of tension and release, and 'Capacitor' uses that pattern with a sharper lyrical edge. 'Worthless songs inside the fame / Waiting for the checks to come' lands with the kind of wry self-awareness that only sounds easy to write, and it earns the song its credibility: this is PCBender processing the grind of creative life without dressing it up. The track opens spare before the chorus at 0:40 unleashes a full-band surge that hits like early Foo Fighters finding their footing, guitars wide and the rhythm section locked tight at a mid-tempo pace that keeps the urgency honest. The hook 'keep it tight, keep it clear / let it build, then disappear' does exactly what it promises, and the late pivot to a label-voice interjection, 'When WAS your last hit,' is a smart structural jab that reframes everything before it. Three tracks in, the album is establishing a thematic argument about creative labor, and 'Capacitor' is the moment that argument gets personal.
