"Beat Lightning" finds PCBender in a cool, head-nodding groove at just over 103 BPM, built on a clean rhythm section and understated keyboards that give the whole thing room to breathe. The hook does the heavy lifting: "the hush is loud, the echo thin" is a genuinely good line, and the chorus rises out of the conversational verses with enough harmonic lift to feel earned each time it lands. The outro couplet, "broke men float when rhythm's tight, just catch the beat and steal the night," closes the track with the kind of worn-in wisdom that fits PCBender's voice perfectly. A highlight.
