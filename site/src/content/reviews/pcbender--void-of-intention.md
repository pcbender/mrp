PCBender opens 'Void of Intention' with a choral swell that drops away the moment the guitars hit, and that transition alone earns your attention: the contrast between the wide, almost devotional opening and the chugging full-band entrance in D minor at a brisk 129 BPM gives the track a genuine sense of arrival. The lyric is spare to the point of being a koan, 'breath, the traveller, guides an empty mind,' and the production leans into that stillness-versus-force dynamic across twelve distinct sections, keeping nearly four minutes from feeling padded. It lands somewhere between late-era Soundgarden and a more spiritually inclined Queens of the Stone Age, which is a fine place to be.
