A waltz in E major at a clip that keeps threatening to leave the poetry seminar behind, 'Mispronouncing Anastasia' is one of the more genuinely clever things PCBender has put on tape. The conceit, a song about the craft of writing songs, crammed with the very devices it names ('the simile a smile, the pterodactyl as metaphor'), could easily collapse into a clever-clever exercise, but the blues-rock swagger of the rhythm section keeps it grounded and physical. That harmonized guitar passage around the one-minute mark does real work, landing the kind of melodic statement that earns the six-plus minutes of runtime. At 6:44 it asks for patience, but the payoff, particularly in how the bridge lets the meter unravel on purpose, rewards the listeners who stay.
