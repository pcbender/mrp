If 'Transistor' opened a door, 'Relay' steps through it into the album's most emotionally exposed moment. PCBender has always been good at writing characters who are disappearing inside their own myth, and this might be the sharpest version of that instinct yet. The waltz time at a slow, deliberate pace gives the whole thing a slightly off-kilter ceremonial feel, like a coronation nobody asked for, and the lyric 'I don't know the person they love / But she's worth more than me' lands with the kind of quiet devastation that most writers reach for and miss. When the chorus explodes at the 0:51 mark, the wall of distorted guitars and the emotionally locked-in vocal hit like early Springsteen crossed with something more brittle and modern. Positioned two-thirds of the way through the record, 'Relay' functions as the album's emotional peak: the point where the thematic argument about creative identity becomes genuinely personal. The repeated 'Not really. Not ever.' outro earns its weight completely by then.
