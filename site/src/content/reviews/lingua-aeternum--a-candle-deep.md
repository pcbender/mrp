In G minor and a swaying 3/4, 'A Candle Deep' builds its thesis slowly and earns it: the chorus hook, 'If never had, nor ever seen / No way to grasp what might have been,' lands with the kind of open-chest relief you get from a great classic rock chorus, the guitars widening just as the lyric opens up. The verses do the harder work of the song, tracing the gap between those who carry an inner fire and those who never missed having one, and the bridge's turn, 'A curse on all who ask, maybe?' is the sharpest moment in the writing. At 136 BPM the track moves with more urgency than its waltz time suggests, and the production keeps everything clear and punchy without flattening the emotional weight. A highlight, and one of their more immediate entries.
