If 'Here to Be Watched' is the album looking in the mirror, 'Moving Too Fast' is the moment the mirror cracks. The distorted guitars and compressed, claustrophobic mix turn the doom-scroll experience into something you can actually feel in your chest, and arriving at track six the arrangement feels like a gear-change the sequence has been building toward. The chorus lands hard, 'I'm losing the game but I thought I was winning' delivered with just enough breathlessness that it sounds lived-in rather than written, and the sudden silence at the end is the smartest thirty seconds on the track. At a punishing pace in a bright major key, the tonal dissonance between the key and the content mirrors exactly what the lyric is describing. It's the kind of overstimulation anthem that Paramore or early Metric might have staked a whole album on, and STAB pulls it off in under three minutes. The silence at its end also does something useful for the track that follows: it creates the emptiness 'Harder Than I Thought' walks into.
