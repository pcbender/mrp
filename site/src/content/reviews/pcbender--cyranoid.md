The title is a real concept, a cyranoid being someone who speaks words fed to them by another, and PCBender leans into that unease with every line: 'It sounds like me, I know it's not / The words arrive, my mouth obeys.' Set in A minor at a measured waltz pulse, the song earns its seven-plus minutes through genuine structural ambition, building from a clean arpeggiated opening into a dense, full-band swell before a guitar solo that functions as the emotional peak the lyrics have been building toward. That solo is the kind of melodic centerpiece that sticks around after the track ends. The chorus hook, 'A shadow's face where I should be,' hits with the plainspoken dread of prime Roger Waters, and the defiant undertow in the vocal keeps it from collapsing into self-pity. A highlight, and one of the more conceptually committed things in the catalog.
