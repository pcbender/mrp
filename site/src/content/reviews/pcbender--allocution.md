PCBender builds 'Allocution' like a legal confession that turns into a reckoning, opening in spare B minor with clean palm-muted guitar before the chorus cracks open into something genuinely anthemic. The lyric lands its best punch in the bridge: 'I offered ease, you asked for more, and still I wait behind the door' is the kind of plain-spoken line that earns its weight, and the arrangement honors it by stripping back to almost nothing at 2:48 before the full band comes back in swinging. At 143 BPM the song moves with real urgency, but it never loses the tenderness the verses need. A highlight.
