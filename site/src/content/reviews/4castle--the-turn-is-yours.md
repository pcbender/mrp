"The Turn Is Yours" runs at a brisk clip in A# major, and 4Castle use that momentum well: the verses start close and conversational, piano up front, the vocal working through lyrics that genuinely earn their cleverness rather than just gesturing at it. The line "I gave you words to live by on a lease / And when you broke them, I just called it peace" is the kind of writing that stops you mid-listen, the sort of compressed irony that recalls early Elvis Costello at his most precise. By the time the full band lifts the chorus, the build feels earned, and the repeated invitation to "follow the trail" lands less like a hook and more like a handshake. A highlight.
