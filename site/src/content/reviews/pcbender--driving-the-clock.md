PCBender has always done his best work when the philosophy lines up with the groove, and at 161 BPM in G# major, 'Driving the Clock' earns its urgency. The chorus hook, built around the central question 'Am I driving the clock, or is the clock driving me,' hits with the kind of anthemic lift that reminds you of prime Tom Petty: simple, irresistible, and slightly unsettling once it sticks. The production is punchy and spacious at once, the guitars walled up but never crowding the melody, and the layered vocals at the peak feel genuinely earned rather than cosmetic. A highlight.
