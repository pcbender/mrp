Where 'Made by Moving' opens with swagger, 'Confused by Your Wires' immediately complicates the picture. PCBender writes the musician's anxiety dream better than most, and the track earns that territory honestly. 'The amp can't understand / the drummer breaks his hand / but no one hears him screaming' lands like a bruise, and in a minor key at a pace that hits harder than you expect for a track this emotionally precise, the guitar work gives the alienation real teeth. The chorus piles up those 'no rewards' like unpaid debts, and by the time it cycles back the third time, the repetition feels earned rather than lazy. Coming right after an opener built around confident forward motion, this track's undercutting of that confidence is the album's first sign it isn't interested in easy answers.
