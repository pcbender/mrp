PCBender builds this one like a folk ballad that knows exactly how long to let a silence sit. The fingerpicked guitar stays close and unhurried despite a tempo that pushes harder than the hushed delivery suggests, and the vocal rides that tension with real control. The lyric does the heavy lifting: 'They fell like threads cut from a loom / Too fine to grieve, too deep to keep' is the kind of line that earns its place in a song, and the string swell that follows earns its place right alongside it. The Idisi mythology gives the whole piece a weight that keeps it from drifting into generic folk-road-trip territory, and the final question, whether the traveler ever left or whether something ancient came home with him, lands cleanly because the song has done the work to make you care.
