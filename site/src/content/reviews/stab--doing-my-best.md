By track thirteen the album has earned the right to say something direct, and 'Doing My Best' collects on that credit. Opening with just piano and a close-mic'd vocal before the chorus hits like a door swinging open, the full arrangement rushing in and making the release feel genuinely earned, this is STAB at her most vulnerable without losing the room. The lyric 'I can come back a hundred times and it still counts as grace' lands because the production proves it, the swelling harmonies arriving not as decoration but as the argument itself. The Emerson and Thoreau bridge is a swing that mostly connects, giving the track an intellectual backbone you don't often find in this corner of pop-rock. Sitting two tracks from the end, it prepares the emotional ground for the closing pair: this is the moment of acceptance the album has been circling since the opener's cracked mirror, and it arrives without fanfare or false comfort.
