Waltz time at 117 BPM is an unusual call for a summer-dread song, but PCBender makes it work: the three-beat lilt gives 'June Gloom' a woozy, overcast quality that straight four-four never could, like the fog itself is setting the tempo. The chorus opens up with real harmonic lift, the kind of chord movement that earns its emotional payoff rather than just asserting it, and the layered vocal blend there is the track's best moment. At three minutes it does not overstay its welcome, and the E major center keeps things from tipping into self-pity even when the mood goes gray. A dependable cut that trusts the groove to carry the feeling.
