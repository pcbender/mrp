PCBender turns grammar into grief and grace here, building an entire meditation on love out of prepositions: 'In is a room / Of is a ghost / With is the road I needed most.' That conceit could tip into cleverness for its own sake, but the waltz-time groove keeps it human, the brushed swing feel loose enough that the lyric lands like a conversation rather than a lecture. The final turn, 'Love is not ownership / Love is not all / Love is the distance we still travel through,' earns its weight the way a good John Hiatt line does, plainspoken and quietly devastating. A highlight.
