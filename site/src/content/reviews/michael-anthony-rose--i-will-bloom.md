Rose writes about dormancy and return with the patience the subject deserves, and the payoff is a chorus that actually earns its uplift: 'tilting toward the light / something in me burns bright' lands with the kind of open-hearted directness you get from John Hiatt or early Bonnie Raitt at their most plainspoken. The verse imagery is grounded too, 'the seed I buried in my doubt / sends green shoots through the thawing clay,' which keeps the song from floating into vague inspiration. In G major at a relaxed pace, with clean electric guitar sitting wide in a polished mix, this one holds the room.
