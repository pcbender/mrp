STAB leans into the SoCal malaise of June gloom with a track that earns its title: the verses are spare and a little restless, riding a clean rhythm section at a steady 117 BPM in B major, before the chorus opens up into something fuller and more chaotic, which is exactly the right move for a lyric like 'all I do is trip and lay in bed all night.' The contrast between those stripped-down verses and the dense, layered hook gives the song a genuine emotional arc, and the hook itself has the same kind of blunt, repetitive weight that early Veruca Salt used to turn frustration into a sing-along. 'My heart's full of hatred but I swear I'll fake it' is the sharpest line here, and STAB delivers it like she means both halves equally. A highlight.
