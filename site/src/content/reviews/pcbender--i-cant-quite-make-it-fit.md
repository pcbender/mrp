PCBender leans into a classic tension: the more you try to force the fit, the worse it gets, and he lets that paradox breathe across a steady 103 BPM groove in D major that never overreaches. The hook earns its keep, and the bridge lands the song's whole argument in four clean lines: 'Maybe love is crooked / Maybe truth can bend / Maybe all I really need / Is to stop needing it to end.' It's the kind of writing that sounds casual until you sit with it. Warm production, a chorus that holds the room, and enough grit in the guitar to keep it honest.
