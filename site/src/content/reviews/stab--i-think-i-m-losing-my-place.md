The album's strongest moment also happens to be its first, which is either a bold choice or a declaration of intent, and it turns out to be both. At a pace that mirrors its subject perfectly, 'I Think I'm Losing My Place' renders the compulsive, fidgety loop of phone-checking anxiety as pop-rock propulsion, and the lyric 'my focus is a mirror that someone just cracked' lands with the kind of specificity that elevates a relatable feeling into something you feel in your chest. The chorus hits with the same cathartic surge you get from early Paramore, tight and desperate all at once. STAB's vocal is the real anchor, moving from a near-whispered confession in the verses to a full-throated belt at the chorus break, and the spoken ad-libs ('don't look, don't check') add a layer of internal dialogue that keeps the two-minute-fifty runtime feeling rich. Opening here is a statement: this record is not easing you in. It's dropping you directly into the anxiety it means to examine, and everything that follows is shaped by that decision.
