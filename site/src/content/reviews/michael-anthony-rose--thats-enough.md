After 'It's in Me Too' stripped away the last of the self-protective instincts, 'That's Enough' arrives as the album's earned exhale. Rose has always been good at writing around a hard feeling instead of through it, but here he goes straight through, and it holds. The lyric refuses every comfortable lie the genre usually offers: not 'wiser for the damage,' not 'unbroken,' just 'here, and not ashamed,' which is a harder and more honest landing than most writers would dare. The song runs at a brisk pace for the subject matter, but the production earns it, opening spare and close-miked before the chorus fills out with layered guitars and what sounds like strings lifting the back half. That final pass through the chorus, where 'pretending nothing changed' shifts to 'pretending you were spared,' is the kind of small revision that rewards close listeners. As the penultimate track, it does not try to summarize or resolve the album's concerns; it simply demonstrates that Rose has arrived somewhere real. The closer has room to speak because this one did the last of the hard work.
