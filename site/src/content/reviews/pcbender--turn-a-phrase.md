PCBender has always had a knack for making a concept song feel inevitable rather than clever, and 'Turn a Phrase' is one of his better arguments for that. The lyric 'the truth will out, but not for free' lands like a good line should, casual on the surface and a little stinging once it settles, and the chorus lifts cleanly, the harmonies opening up just enough to give the hook room to breathe without tipping into bombast. At 103 BPM in D# minor the track keeps a groove that sits somewhere between Steely Dan's tighter mid-period work and John Hiatt at his most conversational, funky enough to move but never too slick to trust. The five-minute runtime earns itself; each return to the chorus feels like a slightly different read on the same material, which is exactly what the song is arguing for.
