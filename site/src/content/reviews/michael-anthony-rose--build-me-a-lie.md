Running at a brisk 152 BPM in C major, 'Build Me a Lie' opens with a blues-stung guitar figure before Rose throws the doors open on a full-throttle rock arrangement that earns every decibel. The lyric does real work here: 'Start with my fear, make the danger clear' cuts through the noise and lands the theme of self-protective self-deception with uncommon directness, and the callback to the childhood prayer in the bridge gives the chorus a genuine emotional gut-punch. At nearly six minutes the song earns its runtime, with guitar work that has the same kind of technical confidence you hear in peak-era Santana, serving the song rather than showing off. A highlight.
