Closing an album that opened with compulsive urgency and cracked mirrors, 'Bring It With Me' has a specific job to do, and it does it with more subtlety than you might expect. STAB takes a genuine left turn here, trading the jangly attack you might expect for something closer to a midtempo meditation, and it pays off. The track has more forward motion than it lets on, but the production keeps everything spacious and warm, letting lines like 'I trust the part of me that returns / the part that stays soft / the part that stays open' land with real weight rather than sentiment. The chorus hook, 'I can bring it with me,' builds through layered harmonies that lift the whole thing the way a late-period Sheryl Crow chorus does, effortless and earned. In standalone terms it is a dependable cut that reveals range; in context it is the right ending, quieter and more assured than the record that preceded it, the sound of someone who has moved through everything the first fourteen tracks described and chosen to carry it gently rather than outrun it.
