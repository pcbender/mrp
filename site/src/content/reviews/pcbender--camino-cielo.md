PCBender takes a waltz in F major and turns it into something that earns its metaphor: 'Camino Cielo, where journeys end and start again' lands not as a bumper sticker but as a line you've actually walked toward. The fingerpicked acoustic and breathy vocal keep the verses grounded and close, then the layered harmonies in the chorus open the ceiling just enough to make the lift feel earned rather than manufactured. At nearly 185 BPM in 3/4, the shuffle has more forward momentum than you'd expect from something this contemplative, and it keeps the four minutes moving. Reminds me of early John Denver at his most plainspoken, minus the sentimentality.
