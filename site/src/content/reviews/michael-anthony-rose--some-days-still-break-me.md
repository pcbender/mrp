Coming directly after the album's most assertive statement, 'Some Days Still Break Me' is not a retreat but a clarification. The honesty here is specifically the kind that refuses to let the anthem stand unchallenged: yes, 'Full-Size Person' said don't mistake worn down for gone, and this one says, quietly, that worn down is still real. The line 'I can talk a good game when the light is right' does more work than a whole verse of most songs, and the bridge, where Rose catches himself turning 'one bad afternoon into proof I am less than,' is quietly devastating. At a measured pace, the production keeps everything close and warm, with the chorus earning its small swell through added harmonies and a fuller low end rather than any dramatic push. The sequencing here is one of the album's shrewdest moves: placing a song this vulnerable after a song this declarative prevents the record from curdling into self-help. It will mean something different to you on a bad morning than it does right now.
