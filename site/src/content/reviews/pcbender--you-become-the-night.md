PCBender builds 'You Become the Night' the patient way: clean guitars holding space through the verses before the chorus opens up and earns every second of its width. That lift into 'the fears overtake but the darkness provides' is the kind of moment where the production and the lyric arrive at the same time, and it hits. The third verse, where a mentor's vague advice becomes a years-long echo described as 'both blessing and a shroud,' gives the song a specificity that keeps it from drifting into generic rock-night-soul territory. At just over five minutes in E major with a driving pulse, it knows what it is and doesn't waste the space.
