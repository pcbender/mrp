Seven minutes in 3/4 time is a bet most writers don't have the material to cover, but PCBender earns every bar of it here. The album has moved through freedom, law, love, God, death, desire, difference, and meaning; 'Sovereign' arrives as a declaration that all of it, the argument and the searching, belongs to the self who did the searching. The track opens on a single reverb-soaked electric guitar, patient and wide, before the full band arrives and tips the whole thing into something closer to an anthem, the kind of lift that recalls early Tom Petty finding his footing between tenderness and defiance. The lyric does the heaviest lifting: 'I am sovereign / by my own / unborrowed hand' lands as a genuine credo rather than a bumper sticker, and the waltz feel gives the chorus a gravitational pull that a straight 4/4 would have flattened. PCBender knows when to let the room breathe and when to fill it, and at seven-plus minutes he never outstays his welcome. As a closer, it doesn't so much end the album as conclude it.
