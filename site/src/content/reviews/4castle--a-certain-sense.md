The title pays off: 'crossing with a certain sense of uncertainty' is the kind of line that earns its place in a chorus, and 4Castle build the whole track around that tension with discipline. At 117 BPM in F major, the song moves with real urgency, and the pre-chorus swell into the big harmonized hook lands the way a good anthem should, with enough room-filling reverb on the snare to make it feel earned rather than manufactured. The bridge imagery, 'the rage of the river, the loss of a lover, the subtle surrender,' stacks up beautifully and gives the repeated chorus more weight each time through. A highlight.
