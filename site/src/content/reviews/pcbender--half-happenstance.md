PCBender writes in waltz time here, 123 BPM in F# minor, and the three-beat pulse gives the fatalism in these lyrics room to breathe without turning mournful. The chorus lift, where the track opens up around 'in every way the end betrays,' earns its anthemic weight because the verses earn it first, the rhythm section staying tight and organic underneath overdriven guitars that feel worn-in rather than processed. The lyric 'half happenstance and beaver broke' is strange enough to stick, like something Tom Waits might have buried in a bridge and called it wisdom. This one holds the room.
