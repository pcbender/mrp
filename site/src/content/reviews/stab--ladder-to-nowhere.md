At 123 BPM in a wide-open D major, 'Ladder to Nowhere' spends its first 45 seconds building on an arpeggiated synth before the rhythm section kicks the door down, and that transition earns every second of the setup. The chorus hook, 'the only way is up on the ladder to nowhere,' lands with the kind of melodic certainty you find on a Bangles A-side, and the bridge line 'the highest step I'm chasing keeps changing its disguise' gives the ambition-spiral theme a real lyrical kick. STAB's vocal sits right on top of a wall of layered guitars without ever getting buried, which is a production choice that pays off clean. A highlight.
