STAB reaches for something genuinely ambitious here, building a creation myth around a raven goddess in G minor at a mid-tempo 96 BPM, and the production earns the mythology: that cold-open of ambient synths and spoken word shatters into full-band impact around the 26-second mark with real conviction, the kind of dynamic gut-punch that Evanescence used to pull off on their best days. The call-and-response chorus, 'I see / I hear / I feel' answered by the chanted name, locks in a ritualistic groove that grows with each pass. The guitar solo section gives the track its beating heart, and the lyric 'she plucked a feather from her tail / and with her oldest voice / sang life into the dust' is quietly beautiful. A highlight, and proof STAB has more range than the pure rock-anthem mode suggests.
