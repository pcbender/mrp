'Haymakers and Body Blows' arrives at the album's midpoint and hits like it knows the job. The lead vocal cuts clean above a punchy rhythm section, and the chorus hook lands with the kind of momentum that makes you want to replay it before the track even finishes. The countdown bridge, 'One, two, don't you let me go,' is a smart structural move that releases tension just right before the final chorus push, and the four-part harmonies on those 'Oh-oh' lifts have real weight behind them. After the grounded emotional warmth of 'Tiger's Eye,' this track is the album's first genuine fist in the air, and it earns that position. It also sets a high-energy bar that the second half has to reckon with, and the record is smart enough to meet it differently rather than try to repeat it.
