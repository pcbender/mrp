An album that opens with a manifesto about living responsively rather than by design is making a bet on itself, and 'Made by Moving' wins that bet. In 3/4 time at a crisp, swaggering pace, PCBender rides a groove that calls up early ZZ Top or a particularly confident John Hiatt cut, and the central lyric, 'let the rules be made by moving,' earns its hook because the music actually demonstrates the point: the guitars shift and lean through the song's ten sections the way the words describe a life being lived. The lead vocal sits clear in the mix, the rhythm section punches without crowding, and the soulful lead breaks provide the dynamic peaks that keep a nearly five-minute runtime from flattening out. As an opener it does exactly what it needs to: it establishes a sensibility, sets a tempo for the collection, and hands you off to track two with momentum already built.
