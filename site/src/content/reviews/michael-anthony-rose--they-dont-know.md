By track six, the album has spent considerable time turned inward, and 'They Don't Know' earns its place by opening the aperture. Rose starts from the outside looking in, observing youth from the remove of someone who has crossed a threshold they have not yet reached, before the bridge pivots the whole song: 'And maybe I was beautiful in exactly that same way, not because I understood life, just because I thought I had time.' That move from observer to subject is the whole record in two lines, and it lands with the force of recognition rather than regret. The song opens with acoustic guitar and a single voice, the verses breathing deliberately before the full band arrives at the chorus with a lift that feels earned, the kind of dynamic move that Springsteen used to make feel inevitable. At a pace that feels faster than the song sounds, there is a structural generosity here that the sequence needed: after two consecutive tracks of close, particular grief, this one offers a wider view and, unexpectedly, something close to tenderness.
