In waltz time at 117 BPM, 'Montradei' opens with arpeggiated guitar before the full band locks in and the song reveals its true ambition: a mythological face-off between fire and ice, named forces Azharoth and Nimrath meeting at some cosmic threshold. The lyric 'Fire crowns the broken sky / And swears to make it clean' is the kind of line that earns its drama, and the five-part harmonies on the chorus give it the weight to carry that imagery without tipping into camp. The guitar solo is the centerpiece, melodic and unhurried against the dense rhythm section, landing somewhere between Santana's sustained tone and early Heart at full stretch. For a band still building its audience, this is the kind of track you hand someone first.
