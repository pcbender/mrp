This is the album's strongest moment, and it arrives exactly where it needs to: after the opening trio has established the territory, 'Some Things Just Stop' plants a flag in the middle of it. Rose writes about aging the way most people only think about it at 2am, not as decline but as inventory, and the detail that lands hardest is 'confidence was never perfect, but it used to come when called, now it waits out in the hallway like it doesn't live here now.' That line is the whole album in miniature. The song opens spare with close-miked vocal over acoustic guitar, and the arrangement earns its swells, building through piano and a full kit without ever crowding the lyric. At a more measured pace than the tracks before it, it lets the listener breathe for the first time, and that breathing room is not relief but attention: everything slows down so nothing can be missed. The quiet coda, 'No, it's not the same,' offers no resolution, just the fact of it, and that restraint is what makes it the record's center of gravity.
