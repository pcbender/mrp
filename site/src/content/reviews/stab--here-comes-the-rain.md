Written in waltz time at a measured 107 bpm, 'Here Comes the Rain' is the kind of song that earns its big moment by making you wait for it. The verses sit in dry, sparse A minor, STAB's voice steady and unadorned over a restrained arrangement, so when the chorus swells at that first drop, the release genuinely lands. The lyric does the heavy lifting without overselling it: 'Could wash me clean or carry me far / Or just show me what you really are' is the sort of line that sounds simple until you realize you've been thinking about it all day. This is STAB working in a more reflective register than usual, closer to early Sheryl Crow than The Runaways, and she pulls it off with enough conviction to make the stadium-sized production feel earned rather than borrowed.
