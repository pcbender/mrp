PCBender has always written best when he's writing from inside something real, and 'And Then He Rested' earns its place in that company. The verse imagery does the heavy lifting without overselling it: 'old applause like footsteps fades' lands with the quiet accuracy of someone who's actually stood in that silence. At a brisk 129 BPM in A# major, the track resists the drag that sinks most road-weary ballads, and the chorus lift, built on stacked vocal harmonies swelling out of a spare acoustic frame, gives the payoff genuine weight. A highlight.
