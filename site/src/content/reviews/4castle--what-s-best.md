The dynamic shift that kicks in around the one-minute mark is the whole argument for this song: a clean, reverb-soaked guitar and a voice alone in the room, then the band arrives like a door blown off its hinges, and suddenly 'I pray to live, I must attest / the rules not fair in this contest' feels less like a lyric and more like a verdict. At 152 BPM in E minor the chorus has the same chest-forward momentum as early Heart, and the production earns its volume by preserving space in the verses rather than rushing toward it. A highlight.
