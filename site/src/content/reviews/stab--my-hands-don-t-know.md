The album's second half opens with a track that picks up the energy the centre just grounded, and the sequence is sharper for it. Running at a brisk pace, 'My Hands Don't Know' earns its keep on the strength of a chorus that lands like a door kicked open: the full band drops in hard after a close, almost confessional verse, and the contrast does exactly what it should. The hook, built around 'I'm a ghost in the world and it's starting to show,' captures phone-withdrawal anxiety with the kind of specificity that makes a lyric stick, and STAB's vocal sells the desperation without tipping into melodrama. It has the same dynamic punch as mid-period Paramore, verse held tight and then released all at once, and the bridge's parenthetical asides, 'the itch, the shiver, the cold blue light,' give it a texture that rewards a second listen. Coming after the album's contemplative midpoint, it reintroduces urgency without losing the self-awareness the record has spent eight tracks building.
