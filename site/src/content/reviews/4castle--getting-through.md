After two tracks running at or above anthemic, 'Getting Through' provides the album its first genuine exhale without stopping moving. The 'I'm good. I'm getting through' hook lands harder in sequence because by now the listener trusts that 4Castle don't throw lines away; when the song earns it with four minutes of patient, specific grief, that payoff means something. The pulse keeps pushing even when the narrator can't, and the guitar solo around the three-minute mark stays articulate rather than showy, which is the right call here. This is a dependable cut doing structural work: it carries the album forward without demanding to be the center of attention, and the one hook that stays with you longer than you expect is exactly what mid-sequence needs.
