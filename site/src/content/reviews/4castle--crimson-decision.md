Running at a brisk 136 BPM in a waltz-time signature, 'Crimson Decision' earns its momentum the hard way: a coiled, palm-muted verse that holds its breath until the chorus breaks open like a door kicked off its hinges. The lyric 'I was never near or far, I was always held within' lands the song's central idea cleanly, the kind of line that sounds inevitable once you've heard it. That lift at the first chorus, where the arrangement swells and the vocal stakes double, is the kind of dynamic payoff that reminds you why big rock production still matters. A highlight.
