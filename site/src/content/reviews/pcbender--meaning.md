'Meaning' arrives at a sprint when the album needs a surge. PCBender is asking nothing less than what makes a life worth living, and the answer he lands on, 'to be unrepeatably living in this fragile, borrowed frame,' earns its weight by the time the full band crashes in around the two-minute mark. The build from spare piano and vocal to that stadium-wide chorus is the real argument of the song, the production making good on what the lyrics are trying to prove. Moving faster than it feels, the arrangement breathes in the verses and pays off like a door opening in the chorus. After the more measured tempos of 'Desire' and 'Difference,' the acceleration here is a genuine jolt, and it sets up the closing track's waltz-time gravity as something earned rather than simply arrived at.
