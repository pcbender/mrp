Whatever comfort 'Cat's Claw' offered in communal harmony, 'Belligerent' burns it down at a punishing pace. The verse simmers with a coiled, clean-guitar tension before the chorus detonates into full-band fury, and that dynamic whiplash is the whole point. In sequence, this is the album's most confrontational moment, and it hits harder because the preceding tracks have built enough trust to make the outburst feel earned rather than cheap. The lyric 'who the hell am I yelling for' does the song's real work, turning a rant about outrage culture back on the ranter with enough self-awareness to keep it from being preachy. At just over three minutes, 4Castle don't overstay their welcome, and 'raise the stakes, raise the choir' is built for a crowd to throw back at the stage. The sing-along is the release valve the album needed at track seven.
