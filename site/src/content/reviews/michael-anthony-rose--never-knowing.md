"Never Knowing" moves in waltz time at a deliberate 86 BPM, and Rose uses that unhurried triple meter the way a good confessional songwriter should: room to breathe, room to ache. The verses are stripped almost bare, electric piano and bass holding space beneath a vocal that sits close in the mix, reverb just wide enough to feel like memory rather than performance. When the chorus arrives and the harmonies stack up, it pays off cleanly, the kind of emotional release that reminds you of early Crowded House, where restraint in the verse makes the lift feel earned rather than manufactured. A quiet highlight.
