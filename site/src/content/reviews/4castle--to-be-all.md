The lyric at the center of this one does the heavy lifting: 'The lake is still the lake. The dock is wood / I sit up slowly. Nothing was a lie' is the kind of grounded, plain-spoken writing that earns its transcendence rather than demanding it. 4Castle build the song carefully in G minor at a mid-tempo 107 BPM, letting the verses breathe in near-stillness before the chorus opens up into something genuinely wide and communal, those stacked voices landing with the kind of lift you get from peak-era Fleetwood Mac or early Peter Gabriel. The final pivot from 'To be all' to 'To be we' is small and perfect.
