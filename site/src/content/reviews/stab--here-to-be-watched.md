Arriving at the midpoint of the first half, 'Here to Be Watched' turns the album's focus outward for the first time. Where the first four tracks examine the private experience of distraction and digital exhaustion, this one looks at performance and visibility, and the pivot gives the sequence a necessary jolt of self-awareness. The hook lands exactly as hard as it needs to: 'I hate how good I am at it' is the kind of line that makes you stop scrolling, which is the point. STAB builds the song in a bright major key at a brisk clip, letting the verses sit close and conversational before the chorus opens up into something genuinely cathartic, and the stripped bridge, where she admits 'I miss my own face / the one that shows up when nobody's judging,' earns every decibel of the final push. It has the same confessional clarity Alanis brought to her best mid-90s work, but the production stays bright and punchy rather than grunge-adjacent. Placed here, it marks the end of the album's opening movement and sets up the harder sonic territory to come.
