The third verse is where this one earns its keep: 'The boots that I wear are worn to the sole / My stride has grown soft with miles to go' lands with the quiet authority of a line that has been lived in, not just written. At 123 BPM in A major, the track never rushes, and the production gives it room to breathe, clean acoustic guitar up front, with those five-part harmonies swelling in on the chorus like a congregation finding its footing. The call-and-response between the lead vocal and the choir on 'It's a long, long, long hard road' is the hook doing exactly what a hook should, sticking not because it's clever but because it's true.
