PCBender opens 'On to Potter's Field' with a bare piano figure and a baritone so controlled it barely rises above a murmur, which makes the moment the choir-backed chorus finally arrives feel genuinely earned. The Am-rooted progression carries a sense of moral weight that the restrained production respects rather than oversells, and the decision to let the final chorus fade without resolution is the right one: you sit with it. A quiet, dignified piece that trusts the listener to do some of the work.
