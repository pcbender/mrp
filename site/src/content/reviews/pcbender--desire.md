After two large, philosophically heavy tracks, 'Desire' arrives at a measured pace and does something quietly necessary: it turns inward. The opening thesis, 'It's the wanting we desire, not the thing,' is the album's most compressed idea, and it lands cleanly because the track gives it room to breathe. The blues-rock weight of the guitars keeps the emotional lift grounded rather than sentimental, and the chorus frames desire not as craving but as a compass. By the time the bridge arrives with 'Let every wish that wakes me be the one I claim,' you feel the song has actually earned that turn. In the album's second half, this is the track that keeps the sequence from collapsing under its own gravity: still serious, still purposeful, but leaning into something more personal than cosmological.
