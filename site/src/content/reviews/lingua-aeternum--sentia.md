"Sentia" moves in 3/4 at a clip that feels more like a controlled surge than a waltz, and the neoclassical guitar arpeggios cutting through the dense low end give the track a precision that matches its lyrical argument: emptiness as readiness, not loss. The four-movement structure earns its five-and-a-half minutes, each section tightening the same idea from a different angle until the final couplet, 'you are not less / you are when,' lands with the quiet confidence of a line that doesn't need to explain itself. It's closer in spirit to Porcupine Tree at their most deliberate than anything the genre tags suggest, and that's a compliment. A highlight in the catalog.
