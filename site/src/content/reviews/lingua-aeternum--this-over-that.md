Starting from a single piano and a searching vocal, 'This Over Many' builds its case methodically, cycling through three distinct emotional registers, each one tagged with its own fraction: the plural noise of many over many, the specific gravity of two over two, the quiet clarity of one over one. The lyric 'You weren't my half, you were my flame' is the kind of line that earns its chorus, and that chorus at the 44-second mark delivers, the arrangement broadening into something close to classic-rock release without losing the intimacy that made the setup worth the wait. At a brisk 172 BPM in C major, the track never feels rushed; it feels purposeful, like it knows exactly where it's going. Lingua Aeternum has always worked in systems and symbols, but this one translates the algebra into feeling with unusual directness.
