At 152 BPM in a bright D major, 'Excess Fiasco' runs on pure forward momentum, and STAB uses every second of its three minutes to land a pointed takedown of influencer culture without ever slowing down to explain the joke. The chorus hits like a sledgehammer wrapped in candy: 'Filter the pain, monetize the mess, burn it all down in a sponsored dress' is the kind of line that earns its own bumper sticker, and the stacked gang vocals behind it give the hook the same crowd-sing lift you'd expect from prime-era Paramore or early Hole. The production is punchy and controlled, with guitars wide enough to fill a room but a low end tight enough to keep the whole thing from spilling over. A dependable cut that does its job and does it well.
