A waltz at 143 BPM in A major sounds like a contradiction, but Rose makes it work: 'Prepositions' moves with a brisk, almost aerobic momentum while never losing the warmth that keeps 3/4 time feeling intimate rather than frantic. The real pleasure is in how the guitar and piano trade the melodic thread back and forth, each picking up mid-thought where the other left off, like two musicians who've been playing together long enough to finish each other's sentences. Clean, full, and quietly satisfying.
