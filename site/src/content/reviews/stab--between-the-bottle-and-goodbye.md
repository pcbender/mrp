Running at a breathless 161 BPM in D minor, 'Between the Bottle and Goodbye' is STAB at her most unflinching, a breakup song that earns its anthemic sweep because the writing does the heavy lifting first. The lyric 'you picked your poison, I've made my choice' lands like a door slamming shut, and the bridge, which pulls the guitars back and lets the vocal stand nearly alone before the final chorus detonates, is the kind of structural move that separates a good rock track from a room-filling one. It sits somewhere between Sheryl Crow's toughest ballads and a Pretenders song where Chrissie Hynde has finally run out of patience, and that is very good company to keep.
