PCBender keeps the lyric framework deliberately spare, stacking a series of 'why does' questions across three verses until the emotional weight has nowhere to go but the chorus, and the payoff works: 'For the love of a woman' lands with the kind of blunt conviction that early Bad Company made a career out of. The clean-to-overdriven guitar move from verse to chorus is well-timed, and in F minor at a tempo that hits harder than its measured feel suggests, the track carries genuine momentum. A blues-tinged solo in the back half earns its place rather than just filling time.
