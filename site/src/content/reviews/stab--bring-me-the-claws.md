A war-council anthem in G major, 'Bring Me The Claws' earns its tension through contrast: the verses move at a controlled, deliberate pace, intimate vocals and clean arpeggiated guitar doing the quiet work before the chorus blows the doors off with distorted guitars and stacked harmonies that hit like a Bangles track crossed with something rawer and older. The lyric 'Betrayed by one, once called a friend' lands with the kind of plainspoken weight that good rock writing aims for, and STAB delivers it with enough cool restraint to make the chorus payoff feel genuinely earned. Play this first.
