"Hana on the Way" opens with the sound of actual ocean waves before that syncopated bass motif pulls you into something quieter and more inward than STAB's usual full-throttle mode. In D major at a clipped 152 BPM, the track rides a tom-heavy groove while dreamy electric guitar and electric piano float above it, leaving room for a baritone vocal that feels less like performance and more like someone thinking out loud. The restraint is the point here, and it mostly earns it; the final verse in particular, where the arrangement softens and the delivery turns almost confessional, is the kind of moment that rewards a second listen. It's a grower, and a worthwhile one for anyone who wants to hear STAB trade the lightning bolt for something closer to a slow burn.
