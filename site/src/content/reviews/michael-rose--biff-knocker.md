Rose builds 'Biff Knocker' around one of his sharpest conceits: the circus as a rigged moral economy, where everyone from the lion in the backroom to the angel on the wire is just working an angle. The big-band arrangement earns its density, with punchy brass cutting through a driving 4/4 groove and a piano that never lets the tempo breathe easy. What sells it is the chorus pivot, where the mirror-bending imagery lands the sucker punch: 'You came for a show, you're the act to me.' It's the kind of rhetorical flip that Springsteen used to pull in his Darkness-era character studies, and Rose makes it feel just as earned.
