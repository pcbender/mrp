"Body of Work" earns its ambition: opening with clean arpeggiated guitar before the full band arrives and lifts the whole thing into something stadium-ready, it's the kind of build that actually pays off. The hook is direct without being simple, and "each flaw a verse, each scar a hymn" is the sort of line that lands harder the second time you hear it. At 92 BPM in D major, the tempo sits right in that Petty-esque sweet spot where the song can breathe and surge without losing momentum, and the layered harmonies that pile into the final chorus give the closing declaration, "I'd do it all again," the weight it needs to close the argument. A highlight.
