The conceit here is elegant: a lyric stripped to its bones, cycling through negation ('No this / No that / No thought / No mind') until the title phrase lands like an answer. In D major at a waltz-like 107 BPM, the track earns its arena-rock ambitions honestly, building from a spare opening into layered guitar harmonies that recall early U2 in their unguarded reach. The chanted 'Sō-hā' refrain does the heavy lifting, and it works, the kind of hook that feels inevitable once you've heard it twice.
