PCBender writes a two-word hook that earns its repetition: 'dance with me' lands differently each time because the verse keeps reloading the stakes, moving from stolen glances to a bridge that asks the real question, 'What if we never let it die.' At 117.5 BPM in C major, the rhythm section locks in tight enough to justify the title without tipping into novelty, and the chorus erupts with the kind of lift that makes a room turn toward the speakers. The bridge is the quiet ace: two lines of doubt dropped into the middle of a celebration, then swept back into the dance before anyone can dwell on the answer.
