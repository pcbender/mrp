"Dispirited Santos" builds its emotional weight on a question almost too large for a rock song to carry: what happens when the divine and the faithful both run out of answers? At 83 BPM in E minor, the track moves with the deliberate gravity of a processional, and the call-and-response between the two lead vocals gives the lyric its real teeth, turning the chorus into a genuine argument rather than a sentiment. The line "we lost the sound of listening first, then mirrored it in you" lands with the kind of plainspoken revelation you get from the best John Hiatt records. A career highlight, and the kind of track that rewards the faithful and pulls in newcomers alike.
