That opening guitar solo lands like a statement of intent, blues-soaked and confident at 117.5 BPM, and the song earns it: 'driving on the fault line of what was and what could be' is the kind of chorus line that sticks because it actually means something. The verse imagery, Spanish towers, amber light, gulls over the California coast, builds a specific geography of longing, and when the whole thing lifts into the chorus it has the same chest-opening quality as peak Tom Petty. A highlight.
