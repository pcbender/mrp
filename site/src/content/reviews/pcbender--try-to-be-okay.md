At 123 BPM in a clean G major, 'Try to Be Okay' moves the way its chorus preaches: with a kind of deliberate, sunny momentum that refuses to drag its feet. The hook lands hard, that soaring 'fly away without delay just for today' stacking harmonies in a way that recalls peak Tom Petty without borrowing his wardrobe, and the funky low end keeps things grounded when the song could easily float off into sentiment. The verses earn their place too, sketching airport frustration and traffic-choked streets with a specificity that makes the release of the chorus feel genuinely won. A highlight.
