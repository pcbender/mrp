The album's strongest moment, and the place where everything the collection has been building toward arrives. Running six minutes in a minor key at a clip that feels more urgent than the blues-rock tag suggests, 'Prehistoric Lies' earns its runtime through a lyric that keeps tightening its own noose: 'a factual fiction, the truth now denies' lands harder each time the chorus comes around, because PCBender has actually built toward it. The verses do real work, moving from cave walls to megabyte faith with a coherence that a lot of concept-driven rock fumbles, and the guitar solo that arrives late in the track, all bent phrases and earned heat, lands the way a good solo should, like a statement, not a decoration. After eight tracks of accumulated emotional and philosophical weight, this is where the album cashes in. That it does so fully is what makes this a record worth returning to.
