The hook here is blunt and it earns that bluntness: 'there's a hole in my pocket where my heart used to be' lands because the rest of the song has been building the case for it, verse by verse in G minor at a mid-tempo grind that keeps the pressure steady without tipping into chaos. PCBender's vocal delivery carries real urgency, and the guitar solo that tears through the back half is the kind of playing that reminds you why the electric guitar was built in the first place. This sits in the same territory as early Audioslave or late-period Soundgarden, protest and grief sharing the same four beats. A highlight, and one of the more emotionally direct things he's put his name on.
