PCBender builds 'Hardwood Confessions' the way the lyric promises: slowly, deliberately, from honest materials. The verses move through a life in shorthand, a summer crush, an immigrant grandfather, a guitar neck that 'hurt like heck,' and the images accumulate without announcing themselves, which is the whole trick. At 89 BPM in D major, the acoustic stays sparse long enough that when the arrangement finally opens up at the chorus, the payoff feels earned rather than decorated. 'Truth in timber, voice in grain' is the kind of line that sounds simple until you realize it's doing the work of the whole song.
