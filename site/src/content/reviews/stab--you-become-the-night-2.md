"You Become the Night" moves in waltz time at a measured 112 BPM, and STAB uses that three-beat sway to let the verses breathe before the choruses open up into something genuinely anthemic. The shimmering guitar work in the quieter sections recalls the more restrained side of The Bangles, and the payoff when the rhythm section kicks in fully earns its cinematic scale. Nearly five minutes of instrumental storytelling is a commitment, but the dynamic arc between the reflective verse textures and those driving, reverb-soaked chorus surges keeps it moving. A highlight, and a reminder that STAB works just as well without words as with them.
