In 3/4 time at a brisk 117 BPM, 'A Dark So Strong' has the sway of a waltz that forgot how to be gentle, and PCBender uses that tension deliberately. The chorus lands the hook right where it should, 'a dark so strong it makes the day obey' carrying the kind of resigned authority you hear in early Tom Petty or prime John Hiatt, and the production gives it room to breathe without going soft. That harmonized guitar bridge around the 2:34 mark is the moment the track earns its keep, shifting from C minor brooding into something with real lift and edge. A highlight.
