The penultimate track on any album carries a specific burden: it needs to sustain momentum without overreaching, and it needs to leave the closer with something to work with. 'Aureolin' handles that job with quiet assurance. At just under a hundred BPM in a major key, the track settles into a confident groove that never rushes itself, and the lead work is genuinely conversational, phrases that curl and resolve like someone thinking out loud. The production keeps everything clean and spacious without feeling sterile, and the bass sits low but felt. After the compressed sprint of 'Jonquil,' the extra space here feels deliberate, a final settling before the album ends. It stands on its own as one of the stronger tracks in the catalog, and in sequence it functions as the album drawing a long, satisfied breath before the close.
