A protest song with real backbone: PCBender plants a pointed political metaphor in a 3/4 waltz feel that shouldn't work as hard rock but absolutely does, the triple meter giving the chorus an unexpected sway before the overdriven guitars push it into anthem territory. The hook lands on 'your bias predisposes / inhumanity exposes' with the kind of plainspoken conviction you get from John Hiatt when he's really angry about something, and the production keeps the vocal front and center so none of the argument gets lost in the wall of guitar. At 117 BPM in D major, the rhythm section is doing serious work underneath all that distortion, and the contrasting bridge, 'A society forged / when a morality is shared,' opens up just enough space before the final chorus closes the case. A highlight.
