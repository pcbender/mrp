The transition here is one of the album's smartest decisions: after 'Moving Too Fast' ends in sudden silence, STAB walks into that silence with nothing but a close-mic'd guitar and a voice sitting right in your ear, and the effect is almost vertiginous. That the full band eventually crashes in at the chorus only underlines how carefully the sequence has been arranged: the quiet parts of this track are genuinely uncomfortable in a way they would not be if they appeared earlier, and the release when the full arrangement arrives hits like a door blown open. The chorus hook, 'right now it feels like standing in a room with my own mind,' lands on the kind of line that sounds simple until you realise you've been living inside it. At a slow, deliberate tempo, the pacing is smart enough to let the anxiety breathe rather than race. In standalone terms this is a strong track; in sequence terms it is the album's pivot, the moment the record stops accelerating and begins to turn inward, and that role earns it something extra.
